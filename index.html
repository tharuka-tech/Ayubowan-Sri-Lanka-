Temple of Tooth

;

The Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic or Sri Dalada Maligawa,[a] (Sinhala: ශ්‍රී දළදා මාළිගාව) is a Buddhist temple in Kandy, Sri Lanka. It is located in the royal palace complex of the former Kingdom of Kandy, which houses the relic of the tooth of the Buddha. Since ancient times, the relic has played an important role in local politics because it is believed that whoever holds the relic holds the governance of the country. The relic was historically held by Sinhalese kings. The temple of the tooth is a World Heritage Site mainly due to the temple and the relic.

Sigiri Rock

;

Sigiriya or Sinhagiri (Lion Rock Sinhala: සීගිරිය,) is an ancient rock fortress located in the northern Matale District near the town of Dambulla in the Central Province, Sri Lanka. It is a site of historical and archaeological significance that is dominated by a massive column of granite rock approximately 180 m (590 ft) high.

Sri Lankan Lanuages

Sinhala (සිංහල): Sinhala is the official and principal language of the Sinhalese ethnic group, who make up the majority of the population in Sri Lanka. It is an Indo-Aryan language with its own unique script. Sinhala has a complex grammar and is written from left to right. It has been influenced by Pali, Sanskrit, and other languages over the centuries.

Tamil (தமிழ்): Tamil is another official language in Sri Lanka and is primarily spoken by the Tamil ethnic group. There are two main dialects of Tamil spoken in Sri Lanka: Sri Lankan Tamil and Indian Tamil. The language has a long history and a rich literary tradition. It is written in the Tamil script, which is known for its distinctive characters and script style.

Sinhala

The Sinhalese people are the largest ethnic group in Sri Lanka, making up the majority of the population.
They primarily follow Buddhism and have a rich cultural heritage, including traditional music, dance, and art forms.
The Sinhala language is their mother tongue, and it has a long history and a rich literary tradition.
They are mainly concentrated in the central and southern regions of Sri Lanka.

Tamil

Tamils are the second-largest ethnic group in Sri Lanka and are further divided into two main subgroups: Sri Lankan Tamils and Indian Tamils.
Sri Lankan Tamils have historically inhabited the Northern and Eastern provinces of Sri Lanka. They have their own distinct culture, including music, dance, and literature.
Indian Tamils are descendants of laborers brought to Sri Lanka by the British colonialists to work on tea plantations. They are mainly concentrated in the central hill country.
Tamil is their primary language, and they have a rich literary tradition as well

Muslim

Sri Lankan Muslims, often referred to as Moors, are a minority ethnic group in the country.
They follow Islam as their religion and have a unique blend of Islamic culture with Sri Lankan customs.
The language spoken by Sri Lankan Muslims is Tamil, but many also speak Sinhala or English.
They have a history of trading and business activities and are concentrated in urban areas.
